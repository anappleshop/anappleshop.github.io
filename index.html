Splitwise Money Pool

A Money Pool feature design for a bill-splitting application that focuses on helping users plan and manage their group expenses prior to their trips or future payments. Subsequent to my travels in South East Asia, I learned to better empathize with the user and iterate on my initial feature design.

Role

UX Designer, UI Designer, Ethnographic Researcher

Duration

First Iteration: 2 Weeks
Second Iteration: 1 Week

Methods

Personas, User Journey Mapping, Wireframes, Mockups

Second Iteration: just me!

Adobe Illustrator, Sketch, Framer

This feature design began as a team project for a senior interface design course in Summer 2019, but evolved over the past year as I iterated the design and pivoted the product strategy based on new research data.

01. OVERVIEW & PROTOTYPE

The Splitwise app

Why

Splitwise's mission is to make shared living and travel easier.

How

Providing neutral, fair, and simplified expense sharing.

What

By offering web-based tools, calculators, and blog content.

Problem statement

It's hard for travellers to plan and manage group expenses prior to a trip. Unfortunately, Splitwise only helps the user split shared expenses after the purchase has been made.

Solution: Splitwise Money Pool

To address the problem space, I designed a money pool feature that promotes and provides easy pre-trip money planning for travellers. Before the trip, users can set up a money pool in their Splitwise group and a money manager will collect the trip funds. During the trip, pooled money will be spent. When the trip ends, remaining balances can be quickly reconciled to each member.

Setting a group budget and collecting funds

Before the trip, the money manager - the person who will hold the money pool and be responsible for group expenses - sets up the Pool. Money Pool is group-based, and during setup, the pool can be applied to the entire group or specific group members. Lastly, the money pool chip-in amount can be split evenly by default or manually set by member.

Adding an expense during the trip

During the trip, the admin of the Pool can add expenses to the Pool. Doing this subtracts from the Money Pool balance and add to the "total spent" of the Pool. The admin can also add funds to the existing balance. The admin can add funds through the setup process.

Reconciling the remaining balance after the trip

After the trip, the admin of the Money Pool will select reconcile expenses. This will not only close the Pool from further expenses, but also reconcile the remaining balance. The admin is responsible for repaying the remaining balances. Again, the balance will be split evenly by default, but can be manually set by member as well.

02. FIRST ITERATION

To better understand the current application, design space and user flows, we began by interviewing and conducting user tests with two experienced and two beginner users. Based on the initial research, we crafted a persona to better tell the story of who we are designing for.

Roommate expense tracking is a significant use case of the Splitwise app

Emma was looking for a simple method to split bills among her roommates and pay friends and roommates back. However, she finds that Splitwise adds unnecessary steps to the “paying back” process which makes it difficult to justify using Splitwise over a reminder app.

Emma has fears when using Splitwise, as her newly acquainted roommates could easily Settle Up or delete expenses without her knowing.

There is an opportunity to cultivate and facilitate trust when splitting bills

Splitwise's settle-up function is based on a trust system and users can remove expenses without the other party knowing. New users are cautious of adopting Splitwise as they may find it difficult to trust expense splitting with new acquaintances.

Initial Design Space

How might we streamline the bill-splitting process, while reinforcing the values of fairness and security?

Initial Solutions

Splitwise Wallet & Splitwise Pay

Splitwise Wallet is an in-app, pre-loaded wallet that users can spend via scanning the app at various partner vendors. The user is required to load money onto the Wallet beforehand, and during group purchases the user will use Splitwise Pay.

Splitwise Pay provides an efficient process to pay friends back. This feature can be integrated effortlessly in the user’s app experience. From the moment the user makes a payment at a vendor, they are prompted to select all friends that they need to split with.

Splitwise Wallet is a feature where users can load money on their Splitwise account, and use these funds to pay back friends or pay at vendors via Splitwise Pay.

SettleUp+

The initial Splitwise SettleUp feature does not allow two-party authentication. Simply put, one user can settle up the group expense without the others knowing, which threatens trust.

Therefore, we designed SettleUp+. Splitwise SettleUp+ expands the current SettleUp feature and forces the other party to verify via push notification confirmation. SettleUp+ also allows users to choose the method of payment, including payment via Splitwise Wallet.

SettleUp+ is an upgraded version of the current SettleUp function. SettleUp+ incorporates two-party authentication.

03. SECOND ITERATION

The following sections of the case study continued as a personal project after completing a semester of study abroad at the National University of Singapore in Fall 2019.

Studying abroad shaped the way I view Splitwise

Catching spontaneous flights to the craziest places in South-East Asia over the weekends and using the Splitwise App frequently with new friends, I had the opportunity to gain product insights from the user's perspective. This experience allowed me to see the app through a new lens and helped me identify disconnects between our first design solution and the user's mental model. It only took me being 7,962 miles away from home for me to realize I had made the mistake of assuming what my user wants without sufficient research, but this experience challenged me to be an agile thinker.

Three Key Takeaways from Using the Splitwise App Abroad

01. Our initial design would require significant stakeholder buy-in from vendors & users, need on-demand internet connection (which some vendors may not have), and involve in-store scanners.
02. Many of Splitwise's user base are travellers; this user segment prefers cash payments when making purchases abroad due to high international credit card transaction fees.
03. Splitwise does not have the core competencies and technology infrastructure to compete in the FinTech space. Diversifying into this space would require significant engineering costs.

Traveling is a large use case of the Splitwise app that I had initially overlooked

In Singapore, the university organized a Facebook group for foreign exchange and international students to converse and form travel groups. I had to chance to speak to a majority of my travel buddies about their background, travel process, and Splitwise experience through this Facebook group. Most importantly, I had the opportunity to observe how they utilized the app to engage and interact with friends during trips.

User persona of a budget-traveller, Jasmine

Opportunity: travel groups often organize shared expenses prior their trips

The following is the typical user journey of how travel groups manage shared expenses. The current app enables travellers to record and split expenses after the purchase has been made. However, many travellers like Jasmine actually plan their purchases ahead, prior to the trip. This pre-trip money organization is an opportunity for Splitwise to intervene.

Jasmine's user journey of managing and splitting expenses during her group trips.

Ethnographic Research Insight

Splitwise's single process framework for bill-splitting does not align with many users' journey of splitting expense, especially when large sums of money is needed for pre-trip upfront payments.

Revised Design Space

How might we help users before their trips to facilitate a seamless management of expenses among friends?

Revised Solution

I came across the idea to pursue a Money Pool Feature after learning about a friend's struggles of taking the role as the dedicated money manager on her trip.

Further exploring this idea, I found that many Splitwise users had similar pain points and wanted a pre-trip pool feature. These users were turning to Splitwise to find a solution for their expense-sharing needs, but unfortunately Splitwise does not provide a feature in this space.

Splitwise's feedback page

04. PRODUCT BREAKDOWN

Value Proposition

Splitwise Pool allows travellers like Jasmine to manage group expenses with ease. Rather than replacing Jasmine's current bill-splitting process, Splitwise Money Pool supports her group's current workflow of creating and managing an initial money pool.

Design Decisions

Common Design Patterns were leveraged to aid user decisions and support their goals. I applied design principles like visibility of system status to support a match between the system model and the ideal mental model.

User Onboarding

When users first make a group in the app for a future trip, they are introduced to the Money Pool feature in which they can help the users in the planning phase of the trip.

Setting Up the Money Pool

Users tend to use navigate smartphones apps one-handed with their thumbs. Main interactions like friend selection and next step buttons are prioritized at the bottom and can be easily accessed. The back button is stationed in a harder-to-reach area, guiding the user forward in the setup process.

During the Trip

When money pool managers open the Money Pool, They are presented with the amount remaining in the pool first. This page provides a clear CTA to adding an expense, without undermining the breakdown of the group’s pooled expenses.

Ending the Trip

Allowing for user flexibility and preventing users from proceeding with an error, such as an outstanding variance, was crucial to me. It was also important for me to provide the user with feedback on how to recover from the error.

Striving towards Splitwise's mission

More Touchpoints = An Increase in Customer Lifetime Value

On the business/marketing side of things, the goal of this feature design is to provide current and new Splitwise users with an additional pre-trip touchpoint. Therefore, when users begin to plan a trip with their friends, they will have Splitwise as a top-of-mind application to support their expense planning needs.

Tying back to Splitwise's mission of making shared-living and travel easier; the addition of a Money Pool feature will assist users though every step of the shared-living and travel process.

05. REFLECTION

Confronting challenges

The Splitwise Feature design taught me to accept my failures, learn from my mistakes and strive to do better. Spending hours on our initial design, I had to accept the challenge of going back to the drawing board. Through this iterative process, I have learned to be an agile thinker and gained insight of the fast-paced, innovative nature of the tech space.

Putting myself in the user's shoes

As a design student, I always knew the importance of empathy. What I did not know was the difficultly of acquiring a substantial level of empathy to drive design decisions. Oftentimes, just asking what the user would do in certain use cases is not enough. Now, I strive to experience products through the eyes of (new) users, to feel their nuanced frustrations; and to not allow any preconceived notions affect my design approach.

Cultural experiences influence how we design

This project is a profound example of how cultural experiences can shape the way one approaches design. Studying abroad in Singapore was a remarkable challenge, but it was perhaps the greatest opportunity for me to understand how people from around the world behave differently, and yet, still manage find ways to connect beyond borders.
